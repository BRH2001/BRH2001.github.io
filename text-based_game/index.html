You wander down Aisle ∞.
The books whisper as you pass.
One spine glows faintly: “Your Life, Unwritten.”

Take it. | Ignore it. | Go back.

As you open it, the pages rearrange themselves.
It’s writing itself — in your handwriting.
“Today, I will...”

Write: “...become fearless.” | Write: “...become rich.” | Write: “...become loved.”

Your skin tingles. Shadows shrink from you.
You feel invincible — until you notice
everyone around you is now afraid of you.

Embrace solitude. | Try to undo it.

You become a legend — untouchable, revered, alone.
Statues are built. Songs are sung.
No one dares speak to you. Ever again.

A hollow victory. Try again?

You scribble over the page: “Never mind!”
The book shudders. Reality glitches.
You wake up… back at the start.

Back to square one. Again?

Gold coins clink into your pockets.
Stock tickers scream your name.
But every coin you touch… ages you.

Spend it all now. | Try to save it.

You buy mansions, rockets, islands.
By sunset, your hair is white. Your hands, wrinkled.
You die on a throne of gold… at age 27.

Wealth is time. Play again?

You lock the coins in a vault.
Each night, you hear them ticking like clocks.
One morning, the vault is empty — and so is your future.

Greed eats time. Restart?

Doors open. Arms embrace you. Smiles bloom like flowers.
But the love feels… scripted. Everyone says the same lines.
You realize: you wrote their dialogue too.

Enjoy the performance. | Set them free.

You live in a perfect sitcom.
Laughter on cue. Hugs on schedule.
Years pass. No one changes. No one grows.
You forget what real love feels like.

A gilded cage. Again?

You tear out the page. The book screams.
People blink — truly blink — for the first time.
Some walk away. Some stay. It’s messy. It’s real.

This is enough.

You close the book. It vanishes.
The library reshapes around you — smaller, warmer.
A new title appears on a shelf: “Your Life, Being Written.”

The story continues… Start anew?

You walk away. The glow fades.
But now every book you pass whispers your name.
You can’t unhear them.

Keep walking. | Go back for it.

The whispers become screams.
The shelves twist into labyrinths.
You run for hours — or years?
Eventually, you become a whisper too.

Lost in the stacks. Try again?

You sprint back — but the book is gone.
In its place: a mirror. Your reflection winks.
“Should’ve taken the deal,” it says.

Too late now. Restart?

You grab a random book: “Yesterday’s Tomorrow.”
As you read, your memories rewrite themselves.
You remember a childhood… that never happened.

Keep reading. | Slam it shut.

You grow up on Mars. You marry a robot. You pilot comets.
The memories are vivid, beautiful, false.
When you finish the book, you forget your real name.

Turn the last page.

The book snaps shut. Your body flickers.
You look down — your hands are ink.
You’re not reading a story. You’re inside one.

Accept your fate.

You feel yourself being written.
A reader, somewhere, turns your page.
You speak their name — and they hear you.
You are no longer real. You are beloved fiction.

Trapped in print. Read again?

You hurl the book away. It bursts into blue flames.
A voice sighs: “Wise choice.”
The Librarian steps from the smoke.

Listen.

The Librarian is tall, faceless, made of parchment.
“Lost?” it rustles. “Everyone is.
Pick a path. Or I’ll pick one for you.”

Accept its offer. | Defy it.

It hands you a quill. “Sign in blood or ink.
One chapter rewritten. One regret erased.”
The contract glows ominously.

Sign in blood. | Sign in ink.

The page drinks your blood.
Your greatest regret vanishes — and so does the joy tied to it.
You feel lighter… and emptier.

Let it take you.

Your body dissolves into parchment.
You feel yourself bound, spine cracked, placed gently on a shelf.
Future visitors will read your life — and weep.
You are now Exhibit #7,341: “The One Who Bargained.”

Forever archived. Browse again?

The ink fades as you write.
“Clever,” chuckles the Librarian. “No sacrifice, no change.”
Suddenly, your fingers twitch uncontrollably — you begin writing… in the air.

What are you writing?

You’re writing everyone’s fate.
The barista drops her cup. You wrote that.
A child laughs. You scripted that.
You try to stop — but the quill is in your soul now.

Cursed scribe. Write again?

You tear the contract. The library shakes.
“How… novel,” it whispers.
For the first time in eternity, the Librarian smiles.
“You may leave. Or stay. Your choice. Truly.”

Leave. | Stay.

You walk out the door — into your own bedroom.
Was it a dream? On your desk: a single book.
Its title: “Return When Ready.”

The library waits. Go back?

You sit at the Librarian’s desk. It bows and fades.
The shelves hum. The clocks still.
You are the new Keeper of Lost Time.
Endless stories. Endless choices. All yours.

The next visitor arrives… Play again?

You curl up between History and Heartbreak.
Dreams flood in — not yours, but everyone’s.
You see births, wars, first kisses, last breaths.

Absorb the dreams. | Wake up screaming.

You learn to weave dreams into reality.
Give someone hope? It blooms in their world.
Give someone fear? It hunts them.
You become a god of slumber.

Claim your throne.

You ascend beyond flesh.
Mortals whisper your name before sleep.
You shape their nights — kind or cruel, it’s your choice.
The Library? Just your antechamber now.

God of Dreams. Dream again?

You jolt awake — but the library is gone.
You’re in a white room. Doctors murmur.
“Catatonic for 7 years,” one says.
The library… was it escape? Or prison?

“Who am I?” you whisper.
The doctor hesitates. “You’re Patient Zero.
The Library Project… it was an experiment to map consciousness.
You never woke up. Until now.”
